African Burial Ground National Monument

290 Broadway, Lower Manhattan · Ted Weiss Federal Building entrance

Where the climb starts — at the ground itself. The oldest and largest excavated burial site in North America for free and enslaved Africans, with 419+ remains recovered from a colonial-era cemetery that may hold thousands. Visitor center, the 21-minute film, and the outdoor memorial. Bring ID — federal-building security.

Jackie Robinson Museum

75 Varick St, 2nd Floor, Hudson Square

Number 42, up a flight. The man who broke the color line, told through artifacts, film, and the bigger civil-rights story — a few blocks north of the burial ground, so pair them in one downtown morning.
